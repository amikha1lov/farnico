Planting for Life – Organic Agriculture

Scroll down to discover the wonders of Farnico...
Farnico is an international Norwegian-owned private group of companies that grows and processes organic, ethical cotton in developing countries for the benefit of nature and humanity.
By partnering up with underserved, honest, and hardworking farmers, Farnico provides 100% certified organic, traceable, and ethical fibre.

"Our loyal customers are proud to partake in Farnico's story of improving the quality of life of the people involved along the process, as well as giving back to nature"

Sergej Nicolaisen,
Farnico Founder

Why should YOU care?

Today, we know that we are in a climate crisis. Already, the way our children experience the World's weather, food, the animal kingdom will be vastly poorer than we, our parents and especially grandparents, experienced.

The pandemic, regular earthquakes, fires, droughts, and floods are some of the ways our planet is telling us that it would instead be rid of the human race. In the last 200 years, humans have done more damage to the Earth than our ancestors have in the last seven million years.

This is largely down to the existing false industrial-era notion that the Earth consists of a pool of unlimited resources for humankind to exploit. Much of what we consume each day originates from the land, the source of all food and fashion items.

However, the soil's long-term exposure to the chemicals, top-soil disturbance and poison has detrimental effects on its biodiversity and fertility. Like a drug addict, it cannot perform without its annual dosage, and its productivity is in freefall, where more drugs are always needed to match previous harvests' yields.

Farmers, the traditional guardians of nature and soil, are also suffering under the chokehold of chemical-based farming

Instead of being farmers, caring for the Earth and growing to the specific circumstances of their land, they have become like chemists in a pharmacy, buying and dispensing an ever-growing dosage of artificial minerals while at the same time being forced out of business due to rising prices and falling yields.

So, conventional farming, apart from having a devastating effect on nature, fuels inequality and poverty in developing countries.

While the consumer is unaware, underserved farmers grow our fruits, vegetables, grains, and natural fibres for our fast-fashion tastes. Unable to afford expensive nutrients or pesticides, not knowing an alternative, the once respected and independent farmers are facing diminishing returns and are forced to leave their lands or starve.
